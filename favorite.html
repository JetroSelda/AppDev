HACKSAW RIDGE

The true story of Pfc. Desmond T. Doss (Andrew Garfield), who won the Congressional Medal of Honor despite refusing to bear arms during WWII on religious grounds. Doss was drafted and ostracized by fellow soldiers for his pacifist stance but went on to earn respect and adoration for his bravery, selflessness and compassion after he risked his life -- without firing a shot -- to save 75 men in the Battle of Okinawa.



MAIN CHARACTERS

Desmond Doss (played by Andrew Garfield)

The protagonist of the film, Doss is a Seventh-day Adventist who refuses to carry a weapon due to his religious beliefs but still wants to serve his country. He becomes a medic and demonstrates extraordinary courage in battle.

Captain Glover (played by Vince Vaughn)

A commanding officer in the Army who is skeptical of Doss's commitment and ability but eventually comes to respect him. He represents the initial resistance Doss faces from his peers and superiors.

Sergeant Howell (played by Sam Worthington)

The tough, no-nonsense drill sergeant who is initially hostile towards Doss for his non-combatant stance but grows to respect him after witnessing his dedication and bravery.

Lieutenant Colonel (played by Hugo Weaving)

Doss's father, a World War I veteran who suffers from post-traumatic stress and struggles with his son’s decision to serve as a non-combatant. His character adds depth to Desmond's motivation and background.

Smitty Ryker (played by Luke Bracey)

A fellow soldier and one of the skeptics of Doss’s abilities, he initially challenges Doss's resolve but later becomes one of his supporters.


SUMMARY

Desmond Doss, raised in a deeply religious Seventh-day Adventist family, is determined to serve his country during World War II but refuses to carry a weapon due to his religious beliefs. He enlists as a combat medic, seeking to contribute in a non-violent way.

During military training, Doss faces harsh criticism and physical abuse from his fellow soldiers and superiors who question his commitment and capability. Despite the severe challenges and ridicule, he remains steadfast in his principles and completes the grueling training.

When Doss and his unit are deployed to Okinawa, they encounter fierce and brutal combat. On the treacherous battlefield of Hacksaw Ridge, Doss demonstrates extraordinary bravery. Amid intense enemy fire, he performs numerous rescues, saving 75 soldiers and repeatedly risking his own life without ever carrying a weapon.

Doss's remarkable heroism earns him the Medal of Honor, awarded by President Truman. The film concludes by honoring his legacy, showcasing his courage, unwavering faith, and dedication to helping others despite the extreme adversity he faced.

The film, directed by Mel Gibson, is noted for its intense battle scenes and emotional depth, highlighting the extraordinary acts of heroism performed by a man driven by conviction and compassion.


Directed by Mel Gibson

Known for his work on both sides of the camera, Gibson is a filmmaker and actor with a diverse filmography. He has directed other notable films such as Braveheart, The Passion of the Christ, and Apocalypto. In Hacksaw Ridge, Gibson's direction is praised for its intense and gripping portrayal of Desmond Doss's story, blending dramatic narrative with harrowing combat scenes.
